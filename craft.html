The Art of
Handblock Printing

A centuries-old craft shaped by patience, precision, and human touch — practiced by artisans who carry forward a living heritage.

Handblock printing is not a process, but a rhythm. Time, repetition, and focus define the craft. Every stage depends on the artisan’s hand.

“In handblock printing, the hand leads — the fabric follows.”

01

Carving the Foundation

It begins with seasoned teak wood. Master carvers chisel intricate motifs inspired by nature, culture, and geometry. A single block can take days to carve, and one error affects the entire print.

02

Preparing the Colours

Colors are mixed by hand, balancing shade, density, and consistency. This knowledge is passed orally across generations, ensuring that every hue of indigo, madder, and turmeric is true to our heritage.

03

Where the Craft Comes Alive

Fabric is stretched on long printing tables. The artisan stamps repeatedly with perfect alignment—a rhythm of hands, breath, and focus.

No machines. No shortcuts. Just practiced hands.

04

Sun, Time, and Trust

The sun acts as a natural fixer. We dry our fabrics in the open air, respecting the weather and the seasons. It is a lesson in patience over speed.

05

Every Piece, Carefully Reviewed

We inspect every yard manually. We accept minor variations as proof of the hand, but we reject anything that compromises the integrity of the fabric.

The Beauty of the Handmade

No two prints are identical. These variations are signatures—proof that a human being created this.

“Perfection belongs to machines. Character belongs to hands.”
